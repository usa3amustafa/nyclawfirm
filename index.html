Commercial Litigation

We win when our clients win. We meet our clients’ objectives by choosing efficient solutions whether it is pre-trial settlement or trial. Our trial attorneys zealously help clients resolve all types of business disputes, including shareholder and partnership disputes, business fraud, breach of contract, directors and officers liability, tortious interference, and business wind downs (liquidation, bankruptcy, or dissolution). Although it can be more cost-effective to reach negotiated settlements to business disputes, we are always prepared to litigate to achieve the best outcome.

Business Formation

We advise clients on forming and qualifying business entities (e.g., Limited Liability Company, corporation, partnership), draft by-laws, declarations, and other essential documents, and negotiate and prepare partnership agreements, shareholder agreements, and licensing agreements.

New York City Employment Lawyers

Despite the fact that workplaces in New York are governed by myriad federal, state, and local laws, employment-related disputes are commonplace. From wage and hour violations to wrongful termination, we are committed to protecting the rights of employees and helping employers fulfill their obligations. Our employment law practice is focused on:

Wage and Hour Claims

Our attorneys handle minimum wage violations, employee misclassification, unpaid overtime, commissions, bonuses, prevailing wage disputes, and other claims brought under the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) and New York State Labor Law.

Wrongful Termination/Retaliation

Although New York is an “at-will” employment state, workers cannot be terminated for discriminatory or unlawful reasons or in breach of an employment contract.

Severance Agreements

Our employment lawyers regularly review, negotiate, and prepare severance agreements, including non-compete and non-solicit clauses and waivers.

Workers’ Compensation

Workers who have been injured on the job are covered under the state worker’s compensation program and protected from employment retaliation for pursuing a worker’s comp claim (e.g., denial of benefits, reduction of hours/pay, demotion, termination). We regularly help clients obtain the benefits they deserve and represent them in cases of retaliation.
